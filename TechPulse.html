CTO Synopsis

This week brought one unexpected challenges. As expected from the prior week's observation, our new Node developer, John Smith, had a difficult time assessing his workload this week, and thus was over commited. As a result, a buggy patch was sent to QA last week, and our QA team failed to catch a database validation error. This patch was pushed to roduction Saturday morning, which resulted in over 1000 database write errors. PagerDuty successfully notified me of the errors, and I reverted to the old patch within 20 minutes of the event. I will be making a report of suggestions for our new developer, and our QA tester, along with writing tests against the bugs that slipped through. Our other new developer, Mark Jones successfully completed his weekly sprint commitments, although he was up late Saturday night to do so.
Despite the errors on Saturday, our stack had a 99.997% overall uptime. Our app suffered an outage Wednesday afternoon at 12:45:46 GMT, due to a Level3 failure at the Rackspace data center. The HAProxy load balancer dropped for 32 seconds Friday night at 21:57:38 GMT, so I will be adding a measurement of the uptime of that server to this report next week. All feature goals and bug fixes in this week's sprint were completed on time, and are in QA this week. I do not anticipate any issues, so the video profile upload feature many customers are asking for, should launch on time next Friday.
